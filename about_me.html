About my pseudonym, Zhuō Tóng (拙童)

As a Western student of Daoism, I wanted a pseudonym as a keepsake of the ideas I study. While I do not speak or read Chinese, I chose the name Zhuō Tóng (拙童) after learning of its meaning, which I find helpful to keep in mind.

The first part, 拙 (zhuō), can be translated as 'clumsy,' 'unskilled,' or 'unpolished.' For me, it points to the honest quality of plain wood before it is shaped by a tool. It's a reminder to embrace simplicity in my writing and thinking.

The second part, 童 (tóng), means 'child.' It encourages me to stay open, to keep asking questions, and see the world without preconception, like a beginner.

Together, 'Clumsy Child' is a note to myself to write from a place of not-knowing.

░ Zhuō Tóng (拙童), 2025
